Experiment 2 - Living Impressions

Description

My main good was to approximate all the shapes and color of my sample image. I also wanted to try to capture the unique colors near the top of the image, the flowers, and the mountain range in the background.

Technical

The simplest part is the two sloping side mountains which are given a random starting height and simply slope to the bottom of the screen and the grass landscape which is just a rect. The flowers were made by mapping noise onto the grass landscape which created clumps of ellipses which approximate the flowers. They get smaller the further from the bottom of the screen they are to simulate distance. The clouds are just some ellipses that scroll in a loop across the top of the screen. Finally is the mountain range. I simply loop over all the x values from left the right and feed my seed multiplied by my x value at every point into the noise function. Depending on the output I would either increase or decrease the y value then plot a vertex. Since the noise function uses perlin noise this created consitant slopes for the mountain which I was very pleased about. Finally was the snow which was a good bit more difficult. I searched around for a bit and found the loadPixels which would give me the array of all pixels by RGBA values. I could then test if a pixel above a certain height on the screen was the color of the mountain range and if it was, change it to white so it looks like snow just on the peaks. There is also an extra bit of noise so it's not just a straight line across the screen and instead appears more natural.

Reflection

I had a lot of fun with this project. I got to experiment and mess around with p5 js which made me want to push my limits a bit. I don't think I did anything crazy but I was happy about my use of noise and my ability to create something that looked somewhat close to my starting image (if I squint really really really hard).
